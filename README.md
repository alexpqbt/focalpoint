# Focalpoint

> Bringing the presentation closer to every student.

Focalpoint is an open-source classroom accessibility tool that allows students to view a presenter's screen in real-time directly on their own devices — no special application required, just a browser.

Designed with students with visual difficulties in mind, Focalpoint gives every student a personal view of the presentation they can zoom into freely and independently, without disrupting the class or requiring any setup from the student beyond scanning a QR code.

The system runs entirely on a local network with no internet connection required, making it suitable for any classroom environment regardless of connectivity. It is lightweight, affordable to deploy, and built entirely on free and open-source software.

_This project was developed as a capstone research project exploring the intersection of real-time communication technology and classroom accessibility in the Philippine higher education context._

## The Problem

Traditional classroom projectors display one image on one wall. For students with visual difficulties, distance, angle, and lighting conditions make reading slides a constant struggle. Existing solutions are either expensive, require software installation, or depend on stable internet connectivity — none of which are reliable assumptions in most Philippine university classrooms.

## The Solution

Focalpoint turns any device with a browser into a personal window to the presentation. The presenter shares their screen once. Every student sees it instantly on their own phone, tablet, or laptop — zoomable, fullscreen, and completely independent of what anyone else in the room is doing.
